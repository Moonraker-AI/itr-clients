# Psychedelic Integration — Free Online Peer Support Resources

## 🛑 IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER: Not Medical or Legal Advice 🛑

This resource list is for informational and peer-support purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional medical, psychological, or legal advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

- **No Patient-Client Relationship:** No therapeutic or client-patient relationship is formed by viewing or acting on this information.
- **Consult a Professional:** Always seek the advice of a qualified healthcare provider or licensed mental health professional, including your current individual therapist, with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition or mental health concern.
- **Legality:** The legality of psychedelic substances varies by jurisdiction. This document does not encourage or condone the use of illegal substances.

## Notes on Scope

This document focuses on free and community-based online options (peer support, hotlines, community circles, forums). It intentionally excludes paid coaching, private therapy sessions, and underground/illegal sourcing resources.

## Purpose

A curated, practical list of free (or donation-based/free-to-attend) online groups, communities, hotlines, and resources people commonly use to integrate psychedelic therapy or personal psychedelic experiences. This list is not exhaustive — the psychedelic community is large and constantly changing — but it collects active, reputable gatherings and tools you can use immediately.

## Quick-start (If You're in Distress Right Now)

If you are in immediate danger or having thoughts of harming yourself or others, call local emergency services immediately. In the U.S., call or text **988** for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.

If your experience feels overwhelming but not life-threatening, consider contacting a psychedelic peer-support hotline listed below.

## 🌐 Online Communities and Forums (Free or Donation Based)

- **Mindleap App:** Virtual platform offering meditations, educational content, and often hosts free community-led integration circles.
- **Public Discord Servers:** Look for servers with active moderators, clear rules, and harm-reduction channels (e.g., HowToUsePsychedelics / HTUP Discord hosts weekly integration circles).
- **Reddit Communities:** r/PsychedelicTherapy, r/psychonaut, r/Psychedelics — useful for discussion, peer advice, and local meetup postings (moderation policies vary).
- **Erowid / Psychonaut Wiki:** Online encyclopedias providing reliable, non-judgmental information about psychoactive plants and related issues.
- **Understory Wellness:** Monthly Psychedelic Integration Group. Register on-line. Participation is by donation to the Understory Equitable Access Fund.
- **Psychedelic Support:** Offers community groups. [psychedelic.support](https://psychedelic.support/)

## 📚 Free Educational Resources & Worksheets for Self-Guided Integration

- **MAPS Integration Station & MAPS Integration Workbook:** Free guides and a downloadable workbook for structured reflection and exercises.
- **Chacruna Institute:** Provides numerous free articles, educational webinars, and discussions focused on the cultural, ethical, and practical aspects of psychedelic integration.
- **Psychedelic.Support Free Resources:** Free introductory courses on harm reduction and the basics of integration practice.
- **Zendo Project Sitting Resources:** Downloadable materials and guides on peer support and sitting skills.

## How to Choose a Safe Group

- Prefer groups with clear moderation, safety agreements, and confidentiality rules.
- Avoid groups that encourage illegal substance use, illegal procurement, or unsafe practices.
- Small groups (8–16 people) often allow more meaningful speaking/listening time.
- Remember: Peer groups are not a substitute for professional medical or mental health care. If you have a mental-health diagnosis or are on psychiatric medication, talk with a licensed provider before joining groups that normalize skipping medical advice.

## What to Expect in a Peer Integration Circle

- Grounding & check-ins
- Brief sharing in a structured round-robin format (speaking turn: 3–10 minutes typically)
- Reflective listening and supportive questions (it is not therapy)
- Suggested homework or practice (journaling, somatic exercises)

## Additional Places to Search for Local or Specialized Free Groups

- **Meetup.com** (Search: "psychedelic integration" or "integration circle")
- **Local & Academic Societies:** Search for local chapters of Decriminalize Nature, regional psychedelic societies (e.g., "CITY/REGION psychedelic society"), or Student Psychedelic Societies at nearby universities.
- **Global Psychedelic Society (GPS):** Links local psychedelic societies worldwide; many host peer support & integration circles. ("Find a Society" on GPS website)
- **Entheogenic Churches / Spiritual Centers:** Legally recognized spiritual communities sometimes offer open services or community circles that serve as a form of integration support for their participants.
