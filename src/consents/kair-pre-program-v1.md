# Pre-KAIR - Gearing Up For Your KAIR Program

We're thrilled you've chosen to embark on this transformative journey through our KAIR (Ketamine-Assisted Intensive Retreat) program. This guide provides essential information on preparing for your sessions. Please read it completely and bring any questions or reflections to your first meeting with your therapist.

## PART I: Taking Charge of Your KAP Experience: What You Can Control

KAP (Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy) offers a powerful approach to healing, and it's important to understand that it's a collaborative process. There are aspects you can control to prepare and optimize your experience, and also elements that are inherent to the treatment itself. Ketamine's effects vary from person to person, and the specific nature of your inner experience during the session is influenced by multiple factors, including your unique brain chemistry and personality.

So while you can't control the exact course of your KAP experience, you can do things to prepare yourself mentally and emotionally to navigate it in the most effective way possible. By focusing on these and approaching the rest with openness, you can take charge of your KAP journey and work to optimize your KAP experience.

### Preparing For Transformation: Set & Setting in KAP

The best way to prepare for your KAP session is by focusing on set and setting because these elements work together to create a foundation for a safe, productive, and transformative experience. By investing time and effort into both, you can create the optimal conditions for KAP to work and maximize its full potential for growth and healing.

### Set: MINDSET & INTENTIONS

"Set" refers to both your mindset and intentions going into your KAP session. It's about preparing your inner world to navigate the unique experience ketamine can facilitate.

#### Mindset Matters: Cultivating a Growth-Oriented Mindset

"Mindset" refers to your overall mental and emotional approach to KAP. It encompasses your beliefs, expectations, and emotional state leading into the treatment. A positive and open mindset can significantly enhance your overall results.

- **Be Open & Curious:** A willingness to explore new perspectives and delve deeper into your inner world is essential. Stepping outside your comfort zone and approaching the experience with an open mind can lead to unexpected breakthroughs.
- **Focus on Growth:** View KAP as an opportunity for personal development. Focus on preparing to learn from your experiences and use the insights gained to create positive changes in your life.
- **Reduce Anxiety & Stress:** While some initial anxiety is normal, managing your stress levels can be beneficial. Techniques like meditation or certain breathing techniques can help you approach your session with a sense of calm.
- **Communicate Openly:** Discuss any anxieties or hesitations with your therapist. They can provide support and guidance in cultivating a positive mindset.
- **Self-Compassion & Acceptance:** KAP can bring up challenging emotions or memories. Approaching yourself with kindness and acceptance, without judgment, creates a safe space for healing and growth.

#### Remember: A Positive Mindset is a Journey, Not a Destination

There will be moments of doubt or apprehension. However, by nurturing a growth-oriented approach and seeking support from your therapist, you can navigate these challenges and unlock the full potential of your KAP experience. A positive mindset acts like a fertile ground for the seeds of transformation planted during your KAP session. By approaching the experience with openness and curiosity, you're more likely to gain a deeper understanding of yourself and your motivations and maximize insights.

### Setting Your Course: Develop Your Intentions

Before your KAP sessions, it's helpful to take some time to reflect and set intentions in order to optimize your treatment experience. Think of it like setting the course for a journey. It can help guide your inner exploration during the ketamine treatment, maximizing the potential benefits.

#### What's the Difference Between "Setting an Intention" vs "Goal Setting"?

In the world of KAP, both intention setting and goal setting play a role. However, they serve distinct purposes and although they are related, there are some key distinctions.

#### A Useful Analogy

Think of planning a vacation. You choose a specific destination (your goal) and make concrete plans to get there (actions). You know where you want to go and have a clear itinerary. Setting an intention for KAP is more like setting the mood or theme for your vacation. You might have a general idea of what you want to experience (peacefulness, relaxation, cultural exploration), but you're open to unique and unexpected discoveries your journey may bring and allow space for serendipitous moments.

| Feature | Goal Setting | Intention Setting |
|---|---|---|
| Focus | Outcome-oriented. Aims for specific, measurable achievements by the end of treatment. | Process-oriented. Guides inner exploration during a session without a predetermined outcome. |
| Nature | Action-oriented. Involves concrete actions to take (e.g., "Practice mindfulness daily"). | Focuses on "being." Cultivates a state of mind or feeling (e.g., "Feeling a sense of peace and acceptance"). |
| Specificity | Specific and measurable. Clearly defines success with metrics or timeframes (e.g., "Reduce depression score by 50%"). | Direction-oriented. Acts as a compass, guiding attention toward qualities to cultivate (e.g., "Openness to new ways of processing emotions"). |

**In essence:** Your goals define what you want to achieve in therapy (the destination), while your intentions define how you want to show up during your KAP session (the guiding light for the journey).

#### How Goals & Intentions Work Together

- **Goals Inform Intentions:** Your broader treatment goals help you formulate a focused intention.
  - *Example:* If your goal is to manage depression, your intention might be "To access feelings of hope and optimism."
- **Intentions Refine Goals:** Insights gained during your session can help you refine your goals for therapy.
  - *Example:* Exploring past experiences via intention might lead you to set a new goal to address unresolved emotional issues.

(For now, focus only on setting your intentions; we'll address goal-setting later in the program.)

#### How to Set a Meaningful Intention

- **Identify Your Hopes:** What do you hope to gain from KAP? Emotional healing? Personal growth? Relief from specific symptoms?
- **Positive Framing:** Instead of "Stop feeling anxious," aim for "Feel more confident and calm."
- **Specificity & Openness:** While some detail is helpful, remain open to the unique emotions and insights that may surface during your KAP session.

#### Examples of KAP Intentions

- To access new perspectives and creative solutions.
- To connect with a sense of inner peace and acceptance.
- To cultivate a sense of inner peace and calm.
- To gain a deeper understanding of myself and my emotions.
- To release past emotional burdens and move forward with a sense of lightness.

Remember, the most important aspect of setting an intention is to develop something that feels meaningful and resonates with you. This will help you navigate your KAP experience with a sense of purpose and openness, maximizing its transformative potential.

### Write Them Down: Daily Journaling

Once you've formulated your intentions, write them down and keep them somewhere accessible. Setting an intention is a crucial step in preparing for your KAP session. Daily journaling can be a powerful tool to solidify your intentions and enhance the overall impact of your KAP experience.

#### Why Daily Journaling is So Valuable for KAP

- **Clarity and Focus:** The act of writing down your intention helps you refine and clarify it, making it more specific and meaningful for you.
- **Deeper Reflection:** Journaling prompts introspection, encouraging you to delve deeper into the emotions and experiences that underlie your intention.
- **Enhanced Self-Awareness:** Regular journaling fosters self-awareness by allowing you to track your emotional state and thought patterns over time. It allows you to explore the thoughts and emotions surrounding your intention which can lead to deeper insights and a better understanding of what you hope to achieve through KAP. This reflection can reveal deeper aspects of yourself and your motivations.
- **Uncovering Hidden Aspects:** Sometimes, journaling can reveal unexpected emotions or thoughts related to your intention. These discoveries can enrich your KAP experience and lead to surprising breakthroughs.
- **Mind-Body Connection:** The act of using your hand to write your thoughts in your journal adds an additional connection between your mind and body, therefore further enhancing the work that you are doing in your KAP sessions.

#### Some Journaling Suggestions for KAP

- **Set Aside Time:** Dedicate a specific time each day for journaling, even if it's just for a few minutes. Consistency is key to reaping the benefits.
- **Find Your Style:** There's no right or wrong way to journal. Experiment with freewriting, stream-of-consciousness, or bullet points - whatever feels most comfortable and engaging for you.
- **Structured Prompts:** If you prefer more structure, you can use these prompts to help you refine your KAP intention. Examples include:

##### The Core Intention

- What is the one primary quality or feeling I want to invite into the session? (e.g., openness, self-forgiveness, clarity)
- What core challenge am I willing to explore without judgment? (Name the issue: my anxiety, my sense of disconnection, my anger at a past event)
- What am I most ready to surrender or let go of during this experience? (Identify a burden: the need for control, a specific limiting belief, a heavy responsibility)

##### Current State and Focus

- What emotions came up for me today?
- What thoughts or mental loops have been on repeat this week, and how do they impact my energy? (Identify current thought patterns)
- What parts of myself or my life have felt neglected recently? (Focus on self-care and attention)

##### Mind-Body Connection

- Where in my body am I currently holding tension, and what message might that tension be trying to send me? (Connect physical sensation to emotional state)
- What physical action (e.g., deep breath, gentle stretch, moment of stillness) can I use as an anchor if the experience feels overwhelming? (Establish a grounding technique)

**Embrace the Journey:** Don't worry about grammar or perfection. Your journal is a safe space for honest exploration and reflection.

Journaling is a simple yet powerful tool. By incorporating daily journaling into your KAP preparation, you can significantly amplify the power of your intentions and be well on your way to a more transformative and lasting experience.

### SETTING: ENVIRONMENT

Setting refers to the external environment in which your KAP therapy takes place. While the physical treatment space itself will be provided by our clinic, there are some things that you might want to do to prepare and enhance it to make it a uniquely supportive setting for yourself. Below are some suggestions, but remember these are purely optional.

### CREATING A SACRED SPACE

Beyond the medication itself, a crucial element in maximizing its potential is creating a sacred space. This isn't about religious connotations, but rather about fostering an environment that feels safe, supportive, and conducive to profound inner exploration.

#### An Altar

Some clients like to create a physical "altar" in the treatment room to personalize their experience. This is not for everyone; the idea of an altar might not resonate with you. It's your choice.

An altar can be as simple or elaborate as you like. From a single meaningful object to a curated collection, your altar is a reflection of you. It's all about creating a space that reflects your intentions, filled with objects that resonate with you. And you don't necessarily need physical objects. Mantras, quotes, or even visualizations can be powerful tools.

Consider what aspects of healing, growth, or spirituality hold meaning for you in the context of KAP. Can you identify objects, pictures, mantras, or symbols that resonate with your motivations, sources of strength, and life aspirations?

#### Possible Altar Items

- **Natural Elements:** Crystals, stones, seashells, or feathers can represent grounding and connection to nature.
- **Religious or Spiritual Symbols:** If these hold meaning for you, consider incorporating them, such as a cross, statue, or mala beads.
- **Personal Keepsakes:** Photos of loved ones, inspirational quotes, or meaningful artwork can serve as reminders of your support system and goals.

We encourage you to consider bringing these items if you think they'd be beneficial and might enhance your experience. This is your personal journey, and what feels supportive is most important. There's absolutely no pressure!

## PART II: Preparation for the Day Before and Day of Your KAP Session

Prepare for your ketamine session the prior day and night. Refrain from alcohol, marijuana, and any other mood-altering drugs other than those essential/prescribed. Eat lightly, thoughtfully, and only healthy food. If you are already familiar with practices such as meditation, yoga, or breath work, these can be a valuable way of creating an open posture to your upcoming ketamine session. Of course, some anxiety is normal leading into your sessions.

On the day of the session, please try to remember that even though you've spent some time creating an intention for your ketamine work, during the sessions, it's important not to be too attached to the outcome. There is no need to try to control the experience. Be open to whatever arises in each session. The experience of 'letting go' will stay with you for days to come. Important words to guide you through your sessions: **Stay Open! Let go! Be Curious! Allow!**

**Body: IMPORTANT!!!** Regarding your medications, NO stimulants or tranquilizers (Adderall, Xanax, etc.) should be taken on the day of your ketamine session. These may be taken afterwards, if necessary. Check with your medical provider for guidance in this area.

- **Empty Stomach:** No Food or drink for 4 hours prior to taking the medication. You should begin the session with an empty stomach.
- **Empty Bladder:** Be sure to have used the bathroom (emptied your bladder) prior to the session so that you'll have no need to get up for the next 90 mins or so.
- **Nausea Prevention & Zofran:** If you have been given an anti-nausea medication (Zofran), take this about 30 mins prior to the scheduled start time of the session.
- **Freshly Brushed (For sublingual ketamine administration):** In order to optimize absorption of the medicine, be sure to brush your mouth well, including teeth, gums, inside cheeks, and under tongue. Also, do not have any candy, milk, or food that may coat the inside of your mouth in such a way as to reduce absorption if you are taking an oral dose. Particularly stay away from any butter, fat, or oily foods.
- **No Driving:** Please have a taxi, rideshare, friend or family member shuttle you to the treatment center on the days of your medicine sessions. Although you will be okay to drive in the morning, you should not attempt to drive a motor vehicle for the rest of the day following your medicine session. This means that you will not be fully clear to drive until the next day.

### What to expect the day of your ketamine session

#### Physical Setting

The treatment room is designed to be a comfortable place for you to relax, away from any stimulating external inputs, to provide you with a sense of privacy and space to connect as you need to with your KAP therapist. Each treatment room has comfortable seating arrangements, eye shades to facilitate an inward, reflective focus, and a KAP-specific playlist specifically designed for ketamine treatment sessions. Even though it is tempting to bring your own playlists or orchestrate the music for each session, it's actually best for your session to simply 'go along' with what is provided, perhaps providing some feedback after the session so your therapist can find a playlist that best fits your taste.

If you are chilly or simply would like to feel the coziness of a blanket, we provide you with a weighted blanket for you to use during your session. Feel free to bring your own if you prefer.

While the treatment rooms are quite private, vague background sounds from within the building (i.e. footsteps, distant muffled voices, sounds outside the building such as cars, etc.) can be heard. The reality is that we are located in an office building and therefore have to live with a certain amount of ambient noise as evidence of others nearby. With this in mind, please know that your sense of privacy is very important to us, and we therefore have taken precautions, particularly with the placement of sound machines and waiting room music, to ensure that even in hearing voices from other rooms, both your own and others' conversations are not discernible other than as perhaps muffled/distant background noise. Please let us know if you have any concerns in this regard.

**Important Note:** Your therapist will always be in attendance with you during your session with the medicine. While some clinics feel comfortable leaving the room during the course of ketamine administration, that is not our practice. We believe your comfort and safety are critical to your growth with this medicine. We believe it is important to have an experienced clinician by your side to offer a soothing, grounding presence given the likelihood you may enter an unusual, 'non-ordinary' state of consciousness for a short period of time during the ketamine journey. While we always seek to ensure dosing is appropriate to your comfort and experience with the medicine, we know on occasion, it can feel disconcerting and intense. You should, therefore, be reassured that we are here with you by your side during the whole process of ketamine administration.

### STARTING YOUR SESSION

As we have discussed, during a ketamine session, individuals can anticipate a unique and potentially transformative experience. As the ketamine begins to take effect, individuals often undergo a range of sensations and perceptions. Some describe feeling a sense of dissociation from their body or surroundings, while others may experience visual or auditory distortions. These effects are temporary and tend to peak during the session before gradually subsiding. Alongside these perceptual changes, many individuals report a heightened emotional awareness and a sense of introspection, allowing them to explore and process difficult thoughts and memories in a way that feels safer and more accessible than normal.

### DURING YOUR SESSION

- **Duration of the Experience:** The medication tends to be active in your system for approximately 45-90 minutes.
- **Movement & Noise:** You are asked to remain seated until you and your therapist determine that you are safe to move around. Some people feel urges to make body movements (i.e., flailing) and if this is the case, we will be present to ensure your safety. If you engage in any strong movements we will direct you to contain them enough to diminish concern. This is highly unlikely, as the vast majority of people remain very calm and relaxed throughout their session.
- We recognize that making sounds, exclamations, talking, etc. during your session is natural for some people. Your therapist is aware of the offices adjacent to yours and may remind you to bring sounds back within a range that is considered reasonable for the space. Your therapist values your expression and will always attempt to provide for this as much as is reasonably possible within the safe container you have created together.
- **Touch:** We DO NOT permit any sexual touch in our work with clients. The only touch that may be offered is always at your initiative and discretion, and is solely for the purpose of providing a deepened sense of support, care, and connection during your process. You and your therapist will discuss this before the first medicine session to establish a mutual understanding of what you might prefer.
- **Nausea:** On very rare occasions people feel nauseous during the session. This is not a problem nor dangerous, though we want to make sure that if you begin to feel nauseous, that you let us know as soon as possible because we want to make sure you are sitting up and that we can provide you with a container in case you actually need to throw up.

### ENDING YOUR SESSION

You and your therapist will determine when the medicine session is over. This is variable and depends on a number of factors which indicate whether you are fully 'back' to full functioning. You should no longer feel dizzy or disoriented. You should be verbally responsive and coherent, balanced, steady, and ambulatory.

**Bathroom:** On occasion, even if you may have used the bathroom just before the start of your session, there is a chance you may need to use the bathroom again near the end. If you are still feeling hazy/under the influence of the medication, your therapist will help you get up and make your way to the bathroom. Until you get to the bathroom, they may have you stand behind them, placing your hands on their shoulders to steady yourself, or they may simply follow closely behind you as you walk while staying nearby as a precaution. Your therapist will wait outside the bathroom for you and help you return to your place in the treatment room until the session is considered safely completed.

**Integrating Your Experience as The Medicine Wears Off:** The heart of the medicine sessions is the period of integration work (post medicine conversation and therapy) that occurs as the medicine's main effect begins to subside. You may have various insights that occur during your journey. There are many ways in which the strange, 'non-ordinary' aspects of the experience have surprising relevance to your treatment goals. We will explore the potential relevance of your experience, as well as determine possible emotional, cognitive, or behavioral insights that we can utilize throughout the remainder of your retreat.

### HOURS AFTER YOUR SESSION

In the first few hours following your KAP session, it's common to feel lingering effects. Ketamine can cause you to feel dissociative, leaving you feeling ungrounded or disconnected. Be gentle with yourself. Try using the following suggestions to help ground yourself if needed:

#### Sensory Techniques

- **Focus on your breath:** Pay attention to the feeling of your breath entering and leaving your nostrils. This simple act can bring your awareness back to your body.
- **Engage your senses:** Notice the sights, sounds, smells, tastes, and textures around you. Sip on some water or juice, savor the coolness or sweetness.
- **Movement:** Gentle movement can be helpful. Try some light stretches, yoga poses, or a walk outside in nature if you feel up to it.

#### Calming Activities

- **Meditation or Mindfulness Exercises:** Even a few minutes of meditation can help bring your focus back to the present moment.
- **Journaling:** Write down your thoughts and feelings about your experience. This can be a helpful way to process what happened.
- **Listen to Calming Music:** Choose music that is relaxing and soothing to you.

#### Supportive Environment

- **Spend Time with Loved Ones:** Talking to a trusted friend or family member can help you feel grounded and connected.
- **Cuddle with a Pet:** Physical touch can be very grounding.
- **Create a Safe & Comfortable Space:** Dim the lights, light some candles, or put on a favorite blanket.

### Additional Support

If you feel overwhelmed, reach out to trusted friends, family members, or your individual therapist for strength and guidance.
